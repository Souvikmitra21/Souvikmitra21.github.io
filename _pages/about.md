Welcome to my digital space. I’m **Souvik Mitra**, a researcher at the intersection of **environmental science, geospatial analytics**, and **machine learning**, driven by a strong academic foundation and a vision to apply data for solving real-world sustainability challenges.

Currently based in India, I bring experience in fault analytics for solar PV systems, evapotranspiration partitioning using Earth observation data, and climate-adaptive modeling. My goal is to contribute to high-impact research that supports climate resilience, environmental monitoring, and sustainable systems planning.

---

## 🔬 Research Focus

I recently worked on a dissertation focused on the use of **machine learning techniques—Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)** and **Random Forest classifiers**—for the detection of faults in grid-connected photovoltaic (PV) systems. The work addresses both external faults (e.g., shading, soiling) and internal electrical faults, enabling more resilient and intelligent energy systems.

I am now preparing to contribute to a basin-scale research project titled:

> **“Partitioning of projected evapotranspiration under changing climate and dynamic terrestrial environment.”**

This project involves:
- Separating components of **evapotranspiration** (evaporation, transpiration, sublimation),
- Working with **MODIS remote sensing datasets**, **climate model projections**, and
- Applying **Python**, **Google Earth Engine**, and statistical modeling to analyze spatiotemporal patterns.

The research holds critical implications for **water resource management**, **agricultural planning**, and **climate adaptation**.
